A **computer** is a type of electronic device that manipulates information, also known as data. It is capable of storing, retrieving, and processing data. You may already know a computer can be used to type documents, send email, play games, and browse the Internet. It can also be used to edit or create spreadsheets, presentations, and videos.

## Hardware vs. software

### Computer Hardware

The physical components of a computer are referred to as "hardware." Computer hardware is any component of a computer that can be touched. These are the major electronic components that build the computer. The Processor, Memory Devices, Monitor, Printer, Keyboard, Mouse, and Central Processing Unit are all examples of hardware on a computer.

![what is a computer](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/mzunairtariq/technovocal-images/Commuter fundamentals/what is computer/computer hardware.png){: width="700" height="400" }

### Computer Software

Software is a collection of instructions, procedures, and documentation that performs different tasks on a computer system. We can say also Computer Software is a programming code executed on a computer processor. The code can be machine-level code or the code written for an operating system. Examples of software are Ms Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Google Chrome, Photoshop, MySQL, etc.

## What are the different types of computers?

When most people hear the word "computer," they think of a **personal computer** such as a **desktop** or **laptop**. However, computers come in all forms and sizes and serve many different roles in our daily lives. When you take cash from an ATM, scan items at the supermarket, or use a calculator, you're using a form of computer.

So, on the basis of size, there are five types of computers:

1. Supercomputer
2. Mainframe computer
3. Minicomputer
4. Workstation
5. PC (Personal Computer)

**1\. Supercomputer:**

While we talk about speed, then the first term that comes to mind when thinking of computers is supercomputers. They are the biggest and fastest computers(in terms of the speed of processing data) (in terms of the speed of processing data). Supercomputers are created so that they can handle a vast quantity of data, such processing trillions of instructions or data merely in a second. This is due of the hundreds of linked processors in supercomputers. It is largely utilised in scientific and technical applications such as weather forecasting, scientific simulations, and nuclear energy research. It was initially created by Roger Cray in 1976.

**Characteristics of supercomputers:**

* Supercomputers are the computers that are the fastest, and they are also quite costly.
* It can calculate up to 10 trillion individual computations per second. This is also the factor that makes it even quicker.
* It is employed in the stock market or by huge companies for administering the online money world, such as bitcoin, etc.
* It is utilised in scientific study sectors for assessing data collected from researching the solar system, satellites, etc.

**2\. mainframe computer:**

Mainframe computers are constructed in such a manner that they can handle hundreds or thousands of users at the same time. It also supports various programmes concurrently. So, they may conduct multiple processes concurrently. All these capabilities make the mainframe computer excellent for huge businesses like banks, telecom sectors, etc., which process a significant volume of data in general.

**Characteristics of mainframe computers:**

* It is also an expensive or pricey computer.
* It offers a big storage capacity and amazing performance.
* It can process a massive volume of data (such the data involved in the banking sector) extremely fast.
* It operates well for a long period and has a long life.

**3\. Minicomputer:**

A minicomputer is a medium-sized multiprocessing computer. On this kind of computer, there are two or more CPUs, and it supports 4 to 200 individuals at the same time. Minicomputers are utilised in institutions like colleges or departments for various purposes like billing, accountancy, inventory management, etc. It is smaller than a mainframe computer but bigger in compared to a microcomputer.

**Characteristics of a minicomputer:**

* Its weight is minimal.
* Because of its low weight, it is simple to transport anyplace.
* A personal computer costs less than a mainframe computer.
* It is fast

**4\. Workstation:**

The workstation is meant for technical or scientific purposes. It consists of a fast CPU with a large size of RAM and a powerful graphics adaptor. It is a single-user computer. It is often used to complete a certain task with exceptional accuracy.

**Characteristics of the Workstation:**

* It is pricey or high in cost.
* They are exclusively built for demanding job applications.
* It delivers greater storage space, with superior graphics and a more powerful CPU as compared to a PC.
* It is also used to manage animation, data analysis, CAD, audio and video production, and editing.

**5.PC (Personal Computer):**

It is also known as a microcomputer. It is basically a general-purpose computer and geared for individual usage. It consists of a microprocessor as a central processing unit(CPU), memory,  input unit, and an output unit. This type of computer is appropriate for personal work such as preparing an assignment, viewing a movie, or at the office for office work, etc. For example, laptops and desktop PCs.

**Characteristics of a PC (Personal Computer):**

A restricted number of software applications can be used.
It is the smallest in size.
It is meant for personal use.
It is straightforward to use.

## Summary

A **computer** is a type of electronic device that manipulates information, also known as data. It can be used to type documents, send email, play games, and browse the Internet. A computer can also be used for creating spreadsheets, presentations, and videos.

Computer **Hardware** is any component of a computer that can be touched. Computer **Software** is a programming code executed on a computer processor. Examples of software are Ms Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Google Chrome, Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Oracle, Microsoft Word, etc.

There are five types of computer - based on size and function.

1. Supercomputer
2. Mainframe computer
3. Minicomputer
4. Workstation
5. PC (Personal Computer)
